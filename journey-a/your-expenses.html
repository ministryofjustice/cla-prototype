Your expenses

To get legal aid we need to know what you and your partner spend your money on each month. Take time to answer these questions as fully and accurately as possible.

You

Homeowner repayments to a bank or building society. Check your most recent mortgage or bank statement.

Money you pay your landlord to live in your home. Check your most recent bank statement or rent book.

Money deducted from your pay by your employer – check your most recent pay-slip. If you work for yourself instead of a company check your most recent tax bill.

Money deducted from your pay by your employer – check your most recent pay-slip. If you work for yourself instead of a company check your most recent tax bill.

Regular payments you make to an ex-partner to help with their living costs or the living costs of your child who no longer lives with you.

Money you pay for your child to be looked after while you work or study.

Money you pay towards the cost of legal help following a criminal conviction.

Your partner

Homeowner repayments to a bank or building society. Check most recent mortgage or bank statements.

Money your partner pays your landlord. Check most recent bank statements or rent book.

Money deducted from your partner’s pay by their employer – check most recent pay-slip. If your partner is self-employed check most recent tax bill.

Money deducted from your partner’s pay by their employer – check most recent pay-slip. If your partner is self-employed check most recent tax bill.

Regular payments made to an ex-partner to help with their living costs or the living costs of a child who no longer lives with your partner.

Money your partner pays for a child to be looked after while they work or study.

Money your partner pays towards the cost of legal help following a criminal conviction.
